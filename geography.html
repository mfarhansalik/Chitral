Geography

The Valley

Chitral is actually one big valley cornered and surrounded by the Hindukush, Pamir and Karakorum Ranges. The Valley until very recently had one two exits. Both of them were mountainous passes above 3000 meters. The valley is split in two by the Kunar River. There are settlements on both sides of the river stretching for over 120 miles. This makes Chitral one of the biggest districts of Pakistan. These is very little flat land available in Chitral for farming so the people have to innovate and deploy terrace farming. The land is fertile but not like the plains of Punjab. The mountains surrounding the valley are very tall which does not allow Chitral to benefit from the Summer Monsoon rains. While the mountains are green, there has been deforestation that has lead to more flooding in recent years.

The Mountains

Chitral is 76% mountains. These mountains are both a blessing and a curse. Because of these mountains and the difficulty with which they are crossed, Chitral has managed to preserve its culture with little influence from outside. However, they have affected Chitral's ability to upgrade from with in. Most of the roads are built in these mountains. They regularly get hit by landslides, floods and avalanches. This makes Chitral a very difficult and dangerous place to travel. During the winter months, most of the regions are cut off from each other because of heavy snow. Sick people cannot cross the Lowari to go to Peshawar for treatment and help can not easily reach the valley either.
