About Me

The summer of 2015 was a very special one for me. Like all Highschool juniors, I was excited to enter my Senior Year. Not only that, I was chosen to represent Pakistan at the Asian Science Camp. For a Physics Major, this was a dream come true. Little did I know that I would come back a completely different person. At ASC, I realise how being a scientist was not the career move I wanted to make. Rather, I was more interested and far more talented in the Entrepreneurial aspect of building new products. ASC changed my mind and that is where I first heard about Babson College. I ended up dropping all my Engineering focused schools from the Common App and applied to Babson as my first and only choice. After a month of sending in my application, I was accepted and thus began my journey as a Beaver.

During my time at Babson, I took part in multiple events and organizations that made me grow both personally and professionally. I joined AMAN in my freshman year. AMAN is one of Babson's largest organizations with almost 200 members active. AMAN represents the South Asian community at Babson which amounts for almost 10% of the entire student body. While AMAN is active throughout the year, its presence is really felt during the months of April and November when the Organizations hosts the AMAN Show in Sorenson Theatre. The AMAN Show is an opportunity for the widely diverse student body at Babson to come into close contact with the rich culture of South Asia. From showcasing a play, dancing the night out and hosting the best dinner of the semester, AMAN does it all. During my time at Babson, I took part in all the shows organized by the organization.

Apart from AMAN, I was an Admission Fellow at the Admissions Office, Executive Assistant to the President, Spark Tutor for Economics, Babson Summer Study Mentor and an Assistant for the Payroll Department. I was also given the opportunity to go to HEC Paris as an Exchange Student in the fall semester of 2018. At HEC, I thoroughly enjoyed the European University Culture. At HEC, I took part in a Model United Nations Conference after 5 years. MUNs had been a passion for me since High School yet I never got the opportunity at Babson. I was awarded the Best Delegate award in the Security Council.

I returned from Paris with not only fond memories but a startup that I had founded during my exchange semester. I spent the next couple of months building a mobile platform that could solve one of the biggest problems faced by shippers in Pakistan. I also ended up joining Phi Gamma Nu at Babson. Phi Gamma Nu was the newest business fraternity on campus. I went on to become the President of Phi Gamma Nu the following year.

At Babson I have learned the art of Entrepreneurship over the course of the past 4 years. I have ventured into different industries and have experienced both success and failure. I aspire to learn from these experiences and translate them into valuables lessons for myself and all the other entrepreneurs that I meet in the time to come! As I am currently working at a Venture Capital Firm, I hope to make it come true very soon.
